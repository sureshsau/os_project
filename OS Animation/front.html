Explore common vulnerabilities and how to mitigate them effectively.

What is a Vulnerability?

A vulnerability is a weakness or flaw in a system, software, or network that can be exploited by a threat actor to perform unauthorized actions. Vulnerabilities can arise from programming errors, system misconfigurations, or insecure design choices.

What is Mitigation?

Mitigation refers to the process of reducing the severity, seriousness, or impact of a vulnerability. It involves implementing measures like patches, secure coding practices, system hardening, and monitoring to prevent or minimize exploitation risks.
